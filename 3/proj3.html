Project 3: Face Morphing

Part 1: Defining Correspondences

In this section, we manually select corresponding points between two faces. These points define key facial features (e.g., eyes, nose, mouth), allowing us to establish a mapping for morphing. The points serve as the basis for computing a triangulation mesh, which is then used to warp the face geometrically.

Using the points from Part 1, we compute the average geometry between two faces and morph both faces into this mid-way shape. The texture (color) is also blended evenly to create a seamless result.

Part 3: The Morph Sequence

In this section, we generate a sequence of frames that gradually morph one face into the other. The transition is controlled by linearly interpolating both the geometry and the color, creating a smooth animation between the two faces.

Part 4: The "Mean Face" of a Population

Here, we compute the average face of a population by morphing several faces into the mean geometry of the group. This creates a representation of the "average face" for the given population.

Part 5: Caricatures: Extrapolating from the Mean

In this section, we create caricatures by extrapolating from the mean face. By exaggerating the differences between an individual's face and the mean face, we generate a caricature effect, where unique facial features become more pronounced.
